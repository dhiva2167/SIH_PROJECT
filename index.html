Water Pollution occurs when harmful substances such as chemicals, plastics, and waste contaminate rivers, lakes, oceans, or underground water, making it unsafe for people and the environment. Major sources include factories, farms, sewage, and household dumping. This pollution spreads dangerous diseases, damages aquatic life, and reduces freshwater availability.

Soil Pollution happens when land is contaminated by chemicals, plastics, and waste, reducing its fertility and harming living beings. It is mainly caused by pesticides, fertilizers, industrial waste, and poor waste disposal. Polluted soil affects crops, food safety, and ecosystems. Preventing it requires eco-friendly farming, recycling, and proper waste management.
